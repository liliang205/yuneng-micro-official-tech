## Article Summary

Industrial WiFi 5 802.11ac modules bridge the gap between legacy industrial connectivity and modern wireless infrastructure, delivering reliable MIMO throughput in factory floors, outdoor patrol routes, smart city deployments, and mobile industrial assets. Operating across an industrial temperature range of -40°C to +85°C, these modules deliver a typical real-world aggregate throughput of 350–600 Mbps under interference-heavy industrial environments using 80 MHz channel bandwidth. With a standard transmit power of 18–22 dBm per chain and a typical outdoor line-of-sight coverage of 150–3000 meters, Industrial WiFi 5 802.11ac modules remain the most cost-predictable, field-proven wireless enabler for mid-bandwidth industrial IoT applications that do not require the throughput ceiling of WiFi 6.

This guide provides an evidence-based, scenario-by-scenario breakdown of how Industrial WiFi 5 802.11ac Wave 1 and Wave 2 modules serve distinct industrial verticals, complete with IEEE-sourced parameters, chipset-verified performance data, and deployment architecture recommendations for system integrators, OEM/ODM manufacturers, and industrial network administrators.

### Key Technical Takeaways

- Industrial Temperature Range:Rated from -40°C to +85°C (commercial WiFi chips are typically rated 0°C to +70°C), enabling deployment in outdoor enclosures, unventilated factory floors, and direct-sunlight equipment cabinets.
- Conformal Coating & Vibration Resistance:PCB-level conformal coating protects against humidity, dust, and chemical corrosion per IPC-CC-830 standard. Mechanical shock tolerance typically rated at 5G (operational) and 50G (non-operational) per IEC 60068-2-27.
- Extended Power Budget:Industrial modules support 18–22 dBm transmit power per chain (vs. 14–16 dBm for commercial modules), directly contributing to link budget margin in high-path-loss environments.
- MU-MIMO Readiness (Wave 2):Downlink MU-MIMO allows simultaneous data transmission to up to 4 clients, critical for dense sensor clusters in industrial settings where many devices share the same AP.
- Enhanced Coexistence Filtering:Integrated surface acoustic wave (SAW) filters and dedicated RF shielding reduce desensitization from adjacent industrial machinery EMI sources operating in the 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands.
