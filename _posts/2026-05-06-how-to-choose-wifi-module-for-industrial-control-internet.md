## Article Summary

Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) terminals operate in environments that bear little resemblance to climate-controlled offices or residential living rooms. A factory floor with vibrating CNC machinery, a solar-powered remote telemetry unit in the Sahara Desert, a refrigerated warehouse cycling between -30°C and +10°C, or an outdoor bridge-mounted gateway exposed to direct sunlight and monsoon humidity—these are the real-world deployment scenarios where anindustrial control terminal WiFi modulemust function reliably, often 24/7/365, for five to ten years without failure.

Selecting the wrong wireless module for an IIoT terminal is not a minor inconvenience. It leads to intermittent connectivity, data loss, field recall costs, and in safety-critical applications, potential equipment damage or operational shutdown. Unlike consumer electronics where a brief disconnection means reloading a web page, anindustrial control terminal WiFifailure can halt an entire production line or cause a remote SCADA system to lose contact with field sensors for hours.
