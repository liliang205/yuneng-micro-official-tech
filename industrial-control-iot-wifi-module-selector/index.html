Selection Rationale

Industrial WiFi 5 — SDIO, Dual-Band, -40°C to +85°C
Your outdoor sheltered cabinet deployment with moderate throughput (1-50 Mbps) and mid-range MCU host is best served by an industrial-grade WiFi 5 module via SDIO interface. WiFi 5 provides adequate throughput at 38% lower module cost than WiFi 6, and the industrial temperature rating ensures reliable operation across the -20°C to +60°C ambient range with safety margin.

Key Engineering Findings

Cost Recovery Timeline

The 2.5-4x upfront premium for industrial-grade modules is typically recovered within 18-24 months through reduced maintenance interventions. Field data from 87 facilities shows industrial-grade MTBFF of 8.1 years vs 1.7 years for commercial-grade in same conditions.

WiFi 5 Remains the Baseline

For the majority of IIoT terminals in 2026, WiFi 5 (802.11ac) provides the best price-performance balance. WiFi 6 justified only for high-density deployments (32+ clients/AP) or future-proofing. WiFi 6E reserved for applications needing interference-free multi-gigabit throughput.

5 GHz Is the Industrial Primary Band

With 24+ non-overlapping channels and significantly lower interference than 2.4 GHz, the 5 GHz band is strongly preferred for industrial deployments. 2.4 GHz reserved for range-critical applications with modest throughput requirements (< 1 Mbps).

Interface Selection Drives BOM

SDIO dominates Linux-based gateways (50-208 Mbps). UART remains relevant for low-power MCU designs with AT command simplicity (1-3 Mbps). PCIe only for edge computing nodes needing multi-gigabit throughput. The wrong interface choice can inflate BOM by 20-35% through unnecessary processor upgrades.
