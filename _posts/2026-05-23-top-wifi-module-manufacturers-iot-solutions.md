## Article Summary

Who This Guide is For:IoT device engineers, product managers, procurement specialists, and anyone selecting WiFi modules for smart devices.

Core Questions Answered:How to select the right WiFi module manufacturer? What are the real-world differences between WiFi 6, 6E, and 7? What are each manufacturer’s unique strengths?

### Key Technical Takeaways

- Form Factor:Compact modules (SIP, LGA, or chip-scale packaging) are essential for wearables and space-constrained IoT devices.
- Power Efficiency:Battery-powered devices demand modules with advanced power management. WiFi 6’s Target Wake Time (TWT) can reduce power consumption by up to 60% compared to WiFi 5.
- Security Features:WPA3 encryption, secure boot, and hardware security modules (HSM) are non-negotiable for enterprise and medical applications.
- Regulatory Certifications:FCC Part 15, CE RED, IC RSS-247, and regional certifications ensure market access. Medical devices require IEC 60601-1, while automotive needs AEC-Q100.
- Software Ecosystem:Comprehensive SDKs, driver support for major OS platforms, and integration with IoT platforms like AWS IoT, Azure IoT, and Google Cloud IoT are critical for development efficiency.
