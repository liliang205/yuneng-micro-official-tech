Decision Framework: WiFi 5 or Entry-Level WiFi 6?

✓ Choose Industrial WiFi 5 When

  • • Deploying in IP65+ passive-cooled enclosures (thermal risk with WiFi 6)
  • • Running PtP bridges at 1-5 km range (link-budget limited, not PHY limited)
  • • Serving 2-16 clients per AP (OFDMA offers minimal gain)
  • • Building space-constrained PCBA (65×45 mm or smaller)
  • • Targeting first-pass yield above 97%
  • • Annual volumes of 5,000-50,000 units (cost-sensitive)
  • • Requiring 3-4 week design-to-manufacturing cycle time

⇧ Consider Entry-Level WiFi 6 When

  • • Serving 32+ concurrent clients per AP (OFDMA benefit scales)
  • • Deploying in forced-air or actively cooled enclosures
  • • Requiring <10 ms latency for real-time control loops
  • • 50%+ of client devices support 802.11ax (OFDMA clients exist)
  • • Budget allows 50-60% higher BOM cost per unit
  • • 90×70 mm or larger PCB area available
  • • WPA3 compliance is mandatory (government, healthcare)
